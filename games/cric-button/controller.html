📖 How to Play

🆕 Start of Every Match

When a new innings begins, the app shows a LOCK MY STANCE screen with a phone-as-bat drawing that tilts live with your grip. Hold the phone the way you'll bat — the bat tracks you — then tap the green button. A short carousel of 3 quick tips plays before the first ball.

Tip: Need to re-grip mid-match? Tap the green 🎯 STANCE pill at the bottom-left of the play stage. It re-locks instantly and skips the tips.

🥅 Net Practice — Use It

From the main menu, pick Net Practice any time you want to dial in a shot without losing wickets. Each mode (Stance / Straight / Off / Leg / Loft / Defence) shows a green target zone on the tilt strip and chirps when you're in it. Real match thresholds are the same — practice translates directly.

Tip: Hit 10 good shots in a row in Off Drive before going for boundaries in a match. Muscle memory beats luck.

🏏 Hold the Phone Like a Bat

Charging port DOWN = bat handle in your hand. Top of phone = bat toe (the tip). The screen faces you, the back faces the TV. The bat drawing on screen mirrors how you're holding the phone — when the bat tilts on screen, you're tilting the phone the same way in real life.

Tip: The bat blade in the artwork is at the top of the screen because the top of the phone is the part of the bat that hits the ball.

📊 The Timing Meter — Read It Like This

  • After the bowl button fires, a TIMING BAR appears at the bottom of the play area.
  • It depletes from full → empty. Aim to swing as it crosses the green zone (middle-to-late) — that's the bat-on-ball moment.
  • The width of the green zone changes per ball: harder bowler = narrower window. Watch the stars (top-right) — 5 ★ = tiny window.
  • Too early (bar still mostly full) → caught or edged. Too late (bar already empty) → BOWLED or LBW.
Tip: Watch the TV's ball animation AND glance at the timing bar — they're synchronized.

↔️ How to Swing — Bat Position Decides the Shot

Your phone's tilt just before the swing = the bat's angle = which direction the ball goes. Small wrist rotation is fine — the classifier is generous.

  • 🛡 Defence: Phone roughly upright (anywhere up to ~35° tilt is forgiven), gentle motion. Always safe — 0 runs, never out.
  • 🎯 Straight Drive: Phone roughly upright, push forward firmly. Best for yorker & good length.
  • ➡ Off Side (right tilt): Tilt phone right by at least ~8° as you swing. Cover drive, cut, off drive.
  • ⬅ Leg Side (left tilt): Tilt phone left by at least ~8°. Pull, hook, on drive — your weapon vs short balls & bouncers.
  • ⬇ Ground Sweep (forward tilt): Tip the screen toward the ground — ball stays low along the turf. Good for full balls.
  • ⬆ Loft / Six (lift up): Lift the phone upward at the swing — like raising a real bat. Ball goes airborne. Big-shot territory.
Tip: Match your shot to the ball type — top-right banner shows the suggested direction for each ball.

🔧 If Your Shots Aren't Scoring

  • Lots of dot balls? Your swings may be too soft — under a real "drive" force the shot reads as a block. Practice the Off / Leg modes to see what force gets a green ✓.
  • Mistimed wickets? Watch the timing bar, not the ball. Swing as it crosses the green zone, not before.
  • Drives going straight instead of side? Hold the tilt for a beat before swinging — the app captures your tilt during the steady stance phase, not at the chaotic peak.
  • Bat feels off-axis? Re-lock your stance with the 🎯 STANCE button.

⭐ Bowler Difficulty

Top-right of the play stage shows the bowler's star rating. 5 stars = world-class — tiny timing window. Lower stars = easier ball.

🤝 Pass the Phone

When a wicket falls, a 3-2-1 countdown lets you hand the phone to the next batsman. Tap I'M READY to skip the timer.

🎯 You're Always Chasing

The opponent team bats first and sets a target. Your job is to chase it down before wickets run out. Pace the innings — settle in, then accelerate.

Tip: Form rises with boundaries and falls with dot balls. High form = better shot outcomes.
